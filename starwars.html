1 | INTRODUCTION

1.1 | Summary

The CW:P Medical System consists of six status effects: bleeding, pain, fractured, unconscious, overdosed, and critical state...

1.2 | Status Effects

As of September 19, 2025, there are four main ways to receive these status effects. The most common cause is when a player is shot. The second cause is through fall damage, the third cause is when occupying an exploding vehicle, and the fourth is by a lightsaber.

  1. Critical State - 30% chance to occur once your blood reaches 0 No medical aid can be rendered Can be fixed by having a second person place you into a bacta tank

  2. Bleeds - Chance to occur when getting shot or caught in a vehicle explosion. Guaranteed to occur when downed.
    Light Bleed • 40s before you bleed out & die/enter critical state. Medium Bleed • 25s before you bleed out & die/enter critical state High Bleed • [Unknown]

  3. Pain - Chance to occur when getting shot or caught in a vehicle explosion. Guaranteed to occur when downed or taking fall damage. Pain slowly disappears on its own once bleeding status has been cleared. Light Pain • 12-20 seconds before it disappears Medium Pain • 14-16 seconds before it disappears High Pain • Caused by severe fall damage • Often associated with unconsciousness • ~26 seconds before it disappears

  4. Fractured - Guaranteed to occur when taking fall damage greater than ~65 studs. Chance to occur when hit with a lightsaber • Disables Sprinting and Rolling; increasing walkspeed by ~314%

  5. Consciousness - Defines whether you’re active or downed. Conscious • Starting status for every trooper • Allows you to use equipment and medical supplies. Unconscious • Occurs when your health has reached 0 • Does not allow you to use equipment or medical supplies • Receiving damage while downed results in death, bypassing the critical state and blood system

  6. Overdosed - Occurs when a player intakes more than 5 Synthnull Tablets • Can be fixed with a secondary person providing a stimpack

2 | CONTROLS

2.1 | Using a Bacta Tank

Walking up to a Bacta Tank will display a hold E to enter prompt. Upon holding E, you will enter the Bacta Tank, disabling your equipment, and removing any injuries/status effects. To exit, you must press E and wait a few seconds while an animation plays. It is recommended to use a Bacta Tank if available to conserve your supplies. You can place others into Bacta Tanks as long as you are carrying them. Location of where to find Bacta Tanks is unknown.

2.2 | Carrying

Pressing V when a downed (unconscious) player is nearby attaches the downed player’s character model to the back of the carrier. Facing your camera forward while carrying someone accesses your medical menu, facing backwards accesses their menu. This mechanic allows you to render aid while transporting a downed player. Similarly, a third person can access a downed player's menu while they are being carried as long as they’re nearby. However, the delay caused by moving while carrying someone causes you to exit the downed player’s menu. A downed player can be placed in an open LAAT if they are being carried. If the downed player is in a critical state, they must be taken to a Bacta Tank, where they can be placed inside. To drop someone you are carrying, press V.

2.3 | Opening the Medical Menu

Pressing U opens the CW:P medical menu, the appearance and functionality is a simplified version of the ACS “Mopmed” system. Pressing U within 4 studs of another character opens the medical menu for said player. Because of this, if more than 1 player is nearby, it may become difficult to access their menu. Therefore, ask them to separate themselves out from other players if you need to administer aid. If there are multiple people downed next to each other, carry one away and administer aid while another person aids the other downed player. If you are downed, you are not able to open the medical menu.

3 | UI

3.1 | Overview Window

Opening the medical menu reveals 3 windows to help administer aid to either yourself or another player. The first window, titled “Overview”, contains: the username of the patient, the blood level, health, and status effects (upon receiving damage). When hurt, your health bar will decrease depending on the lethality of the weapon. For a standard trooper, most weapons will not down them in one shot. Depending on the situation, you may receive a status effect. If you receive bleeding as a status effect, your blood level will begin to decrease. Blood regenerates once your health has reached at least 50%. Below is an image of the medical menu.

3.2 | Equipment Window

The middle window, titled “Equipment”, simply lists the amount of medical supplies that remain in the player’s inventory. This list is purely visual, it has no functionality aside from representing your remaining supplies. The starting quantity for all personnel is zero. You can obtain supplies through the “ . Below is an image of the medical menu.

3.3 | Examine and Treat Window

The rightmost window, titled “Examine and Treat” is where medical items are used by left-clicking the corresponding icon. When an item is used, the progress bar at the bottom indicates when the process is done. An item is consumed when the icon is clicked, not when the process is completed. Because of this mechanic, if you exit the range of another player’s medical menu after clicking an icon. The item will be gone until you re-enter the range of the other player, after which it will be applied to the player. Below is an image of the medical menu outlining the name of each icon.

4 | EQUIPMENT

4.1 | Standard Supplies

All personnel are given the same quantity of supplies. Field medics are an exemption, because of medical items eventually having a weight property; they will have a larger quantity of supplies at the cost of a lower armament. Below is a list of the 7 items along with the starting amount of each:

  • BANDAGES: 10
  • STIMPACKS: 5
  • AUTOTOURNIQUETS: 3
  • SYNTHNULL TABLETS: 8
  • BACTA BAGS: 2
  • SPLINTS: 3
  • BACTA SPRAY: 4

4.2 | Field Medic Supplies [W.I.P.]

Not available until weight properties are released.

5 | ADMINISTERING TREATMENT

5.1 | Bandage

Stops light and medium bleeds. Light bleeds require 1 bandage, medium bleeds require 2 bandages.

5.2 | Stimpack

Returns consciousness; requires blood level to be greater than 10%.

5.3 | Autotourniquets

Stops high bleeds.

5.4 | Synthnull Tablets

Decreases pain by one level, however, taking 6 tablets causes the player to go unconscious. After taking 5 tablets, it is recommended to wait 5 minutes before taking another tablet. Respawning does not reset the counter, you must wait before the counter is reset.

5.5 | Bacta Bag

Restores half of your blood.

5.6 | Splint

Removes fractured status effect

5.7 | Bacta Spray

Restores ~10% of your health.

6 | FIRST AID & CHARACTER MECHANICS

6.1 | Reviving a Downed Player

To tell when a player is downed, their character model will either ragdoll, or their lower half will sink into the ground. Upon opening their menu, they will have the following status: Medium Bleed, Unconscious, Light Pain, any pre-existing status effects, 0 health, and a decreasing blood level. Before aid can be administered to a downed player, it is vital to ensure the safety of your surroundings. Bleeding is the primary concern, the person administering aid should stop the bleeding before anything else. Once it is stopped, if the area is becoming more hazardous, the player should carry the downed player to a safer area. Otherwise, administer a stimpack. If the downed player is low on blood (less than 10%), provide a Bacta Bag before using a stimpack. Once the player has revived, instruct them to heal the rest of their status effects. If the bleeding is not stopped in time and the player enters a critical state, they must be taken to a Bacta Tank as soon as possible. It is important to note if a Bacta Tank is available, it should be used after stopping the bleeding.

6.2 | Fall Damage Mechanics

Ladders do not stop fall damage, catching yourself on a ladder will temporarily prevent the damage. However, climbing down and touching the ground will cause all the damage to be received. Climbing up will prevent the damage from being taken. Rolling does not prevent fall damage. Fall damage is calculated based on your velocity and travel distance, jumping prematurely in a moving vehicle will cause heavy damage.
