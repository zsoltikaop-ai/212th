AIRBORNE COMPANY

212th Battalion — Airborne Company

Aerial Infantry • Quick Reaction • LAAT & Jetpack Deployments • ORBAT
The Airborne Company of the 212th Battalion specializes in high‑risk insertions from LAAT gunships, rapid jetpack assaults, and holding vital forward objectives during large scale operations. Operating as the vanguard and a lightning reaction force, Airborne companies are trained to operate autonomously behind enemy lines and to seize or defend critical terrain until reinforcements arrive.

Mission Set

Primary: Quick Reaction Force — respond to emergent threats and secure objectives via aerial insertion.

Secondary: Forward objective holding, reconnaissance-in-force, and providing ground support for mechanized elements.

Tertiary: Support combined arms operations with precision insertions, sabotage, and rapid extraction.

Operational Doctrine

Airborne Company doctrine emphasizes speed, precision, and disciplined small‑unit leadership. Platoons are organized to operate in loose formations after insertion, using terrain and urban cover to their advantage. Typical deployment methods include LAAT drops for heavy squads and single‑operator jetpack insertions for recon or commando detachments. Emphasis is placed on radio discipline, rapid objective securing, and coordination with air support.

Training & Readiness

All troopers undergo advanced aerial insertion training, survival & evasion, close-quarters combat, and LAAT‑compatible embarkation drills. Leadership candidates receive additional education in rapid decision making, small unit tactics, and combined arms coordination. Periodic company‑level exercises simulate long‑range insertions and sustained objective defense under attrition.

Equipment & Vehicles

Primary insertion:LAAT transport gunships for mass drops and armored insertions.
Personal gear: Jetpacks, reinforced armor, and short‑range support weaponry optimized for rapid fighting in built-up areas.
Support:Light ground vehicles, anti‑armor teams, and expendable UAV recon assets.
Specialists:Demolition experts, recon jumpers, and AT sections for anti‑vehicle interdiction.

Notable Engagements & Legacy

The Airborne Company has a reputation for decisive action on high‑value objectives. Their hallmark operations include rapid seizure of orbital relay sites, cutting enemy supply lines during campaign surges, and daring night insertions behind fortified positions. Their successes are credited with turning the tide in several multi‑regimental operations and have made them a backbone of the 212th's ground strategy.

UNITED:The 212th Airborne Company answers the call where speed and precision are paramount.

DEPLOY: From the port bays of LAATs to the stark ridgelines of contested worlds, they insert quickly, seize objectives, and hold until reinforcements arrive.

ENDURE:Trained for attrition and sustained defense, Airborne troopers adapt under fire — few units match their grit.
